    Governance

    Membership

    Any individual or entity holding a minimum threshold of our ERC-20 tokens ($Rockets) can become a member of the DAO. This membership grants them voting rights proportional to the number of tokens they hold. Members will be able to participate in the governance process through Snapshot, where they can create and vote on proposals without spending gas fees.

    Voting Power

    Voting power is determined by the number of tokens a member holds. The more tokens a member holds, the higher their voting power.

    Core Team and Sub-Teams

    The core team will consist of commited members responsible for managing the overall vision, strategy, and development of the DAO. They will also be responsible for executing proposals approved by the community. Sub-teams can be formed to focus on specific aspects of the DAO, such as game development, marketing, community management, and partnerships. These sub-teams will have leaders responsible for coordinating their activities and reporting to the core team.

    Governance Process

    Any member can create a proposal on Snapshot, outlining the purpose, benefits, and requirements of the proposal. Other members can discuss, debate, and provide feedback on the proposal before the voting period starts. Proposals can include but are not limited to:

    • Marketing and partnership strategies
    • Tokenomics and distribution
    • Allocation of DAO funds for specific initiatives
    • Changes to the governance structure
    • Game development decisions

    The voting period for each proposal will be predetermined (e.g., 3 or 7 days). Once the voting period ends, if the proposal receives the majority of votes, it is considered approved, and the core team or responsible sub-team will execute the proposal.

    Treasury Management

    The DAO will have a treasury funded through various means such as token sales, tournaments, partnerships, asset sales, and donations. The treasury will be used to fund marketing efforts, community initiatives, tournament hosting, and other expenses necessary for the growth and success of the DAO. Treasury management decisions, such as allocation of funds and investment strategies, will be subject to community approval through the governance process.
